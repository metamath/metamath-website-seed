3000 English/Croatian vocabulary flash cards

Last updated: 14-Jul-2009     Author: Norman Megill

If you plan to use the Mnemosyne set of 2500 English-Croatian vocabulary cards, let me know so that I can make sure it has been updated with the latest corrections.

25-Jun-2009:Ivan Štambuk has duplicated and wikified this list with many corrections. I will be updating this page with his corrections, but in the meantime his list should be used as a more definitive reference.

This vocabulary list attempts to collect the 3,000 most important words for use with the Mnemosyne flash-card tool. An unusual feature (compared to some other vocabulary lists I've seen) is that it is sorted with the most common words first and rarest words last, so that (in theory) the student can stop at any point according to his or her desired proficiency level.

This list is primarily based on J. Barefoot's public-domain, 2449-word Master List and the 1510-word Voice of America Special English Word Book. I added additional words from the Croatian Swadesh list, days of the week, months of the year, some additional numbers, and a few other words.

I sorted the final list according to word frequency, using the 40,000 Most common words (TV and movie scripts). The second column gives the rank in that list so that future additions can be placed in the correct order.

To create customized sets of cards, "[num]" and "[cal]" designate numbers and calendar names (days of the week and months), words with "*" are in the VOA list, and words with "**" are only in the VOA list (some of which are used mainly in news reports). For example, keeping only the cards with a "*" but no "**" results in a 1200-card basic vocabulary list. The Mnemosyne set of 2500 English-Croatian vocabulary cards was created by deleting the "**" cards, removing the "*" characters for better appearance, and renumbering the card sequence number.

This list is meant to improve Croatian vocabulary, not to learn Croatian. For that purpose, a phrasebook and an audio course will be useful. In many cases, such as the words "to", "as", "at", and "of", there is no one-to-one correspondence, and the translation is primarily intended to assist a general recognition of the corresponding Croatian words. Finally, the list does not address grammar, and in most cases, only the infinitive and nominative cases are shown.

Croatian is roughly pronounced as it is written, so there is no separate pronunciation column. Some pronunciation guides are given here, here, and here. For more precision, see Ivan Štambuk's list with accented vowels in conjunction with a pitch accent guide.

  • "227" - "understand" is the 227th word in the 40K-word TV/movie list
  • "*" - "understand" occurs in the Voice of America list (the "*" can be ignored)
  • ", comprehend" - "comprehend" is a synonym for "understand" to help clarify its meaning
  • "(pf/impf)" - there are two cases (perfect and imperfect) in the Croatian column, separated by slash
  • "[v]" - "understand" is a verb
  • "2s" - there are 2 synonyms for "understand" in the Croatian column, separated by commas (also, "2w" = 2 words)
  • "razumjeti, shvatiti" - these are 2 synomyms for the perfect case of "to understand"
  • "razumijevati, shvaćati" - these are 2 synonyms for the imperfect case of "to understand"
